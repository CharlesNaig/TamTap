2. System Description

TAMTAP is a locally hosted NFC-based attendance system developed as a capstone project by Grade 12 ICT B students at FEU Roosevelt Marikina. The system uses NFC card scanning, camera-based face detection, and a real-time LAN dashboard to facilitate attendance tracking.

The system is not a commercial product. It is a research prototype designed for academic demonstration and school-level deployment during S.Y. 2025–2026.

3. User Responsibilities

Users of the TAMTAP system agree to the following:

  • Students: Must only tap their own assigned NFC card. Sharing, swapping, or using another student's card constitutes academic dishonesty.
  • Teachers: Must use their credentials responsibly and not share login information with unauthorized users.
  • Administrators: Are responsible for the proper configuration, maintenance, and security of the system.
  • All users must not attempt to tamper with, bypass, or reverse-engineer any part of the system hardware or software.

4. Data Handling

All data collected by TAMTAP is processed in accordance with our Privacy Policy and the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (R.A. 10173). Data collection is limited to what is necessary for attendance monitoring purposes only.

Photos captured during tap-in are used solely for face detection verification — no facial recognition or biometric matching is performed. Photos are stored locally on the school network and are not transmitted to external services.

5. System Limitations

As a research prototype, TAMTAP has the following known limitations:

  • The system operates exclusively over the school's local area network (LAN) and is not accessible from outside the campus
  • Face detection may be affected by lighting conditions, camera angle, or obstructions
  • NFC card reading depends on proper card placement and hardware functionality
  • The system allows one attendance record per student per day
  • Uptime depends on the Raspberry Pi hardware and local network availability

The development team and school administration are not liable for attendance discrepancies caused by hardware malfunction, network issues, or improper use of the system.
